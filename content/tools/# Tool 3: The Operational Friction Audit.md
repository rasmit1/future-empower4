Before launching a secondary offer or a new business stream, use this quantitative scorecard to audit hidden operational friction. Rate your current organization honestly across the four critical pillars of execution drag.

### 📊 Step 1: The Friction & Drag Scorecard

Evaluate each pillar on a scale from **0 to 3** based on your operational reality, not your aspirations.

| Operational Pillar | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | Your Score |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :---: |
| **1. Firefighting Load** | Fully automated core. Delivery requires zero manual supervision. | Minor manual tracking needed. Occasional Slack coordination. | Heavy manual inputs required. Founder or team manually pushes orders. | Chaotic execution. Deliverables frequently stall without active firefighting. | ` ` |
| **2. Single Points of Failure** | Cross-trained team or built out SaaS workflows. No single point of failure. | Small team bottlenecks. Everything relies on your direct brainpower to move. | ` ` |

### 📉 Step 2: Diagnostic Matrix & Go/No-Go Directives

Tally your total score above and apply the corresponding strategic operational directive.

```text
[ Score 0 - 3 ] ──────────► [ Score 4 - 7 ] ──────────► [ Score 8 - 12 ]
  Green Light:                Yellow Light:               Red Light:
  Seamless Launch             Isolate & Pilot             Operational Threat
```

#### 🟢 Score 0 – 3: Low Drag (Systemized Ready)
*   **The Diagnostic:** Your core business engine is highly decoupled from your personal time and manual tracking.
*   **The Directive:** **Go.** You have the structural runway to build out and manage a completely **New Stream** without risking the stability of your primary revenue channel.

#### 🟡 Score 4 – 7: Medium Drag (The Optimization Tax)
*   **The Diagnostic:** Your backend infrastructure can take the weight, but launching a new stream will strain your current staff or introduce tech debt.
*   **The Directive:** **Proceed with Caution.** Do not build isolated apps or hire new agencies yet. Instead, **Integrate** the offer using your existing tech tools as a lean pilot to prove product-market fit first.

#### 🔴 Score 8 – 12: High Drag (Systemic Bottleneck)
*   **The Diagnostic:** Your core operations are highly fragile and rely heavily on manual intervention or founder oversight.
*   **The Directive:** **Hard No-Go.** Launching a new stream right now will dilute your execution capacity, drive team burnout, and trigger revenue drops in your core business. You must automate or hire for your core first.

---

### 🛠️ Step 3: Mitigation Framework (The Red Line Rules)

If your score forces a yellow or red decision but you still plan to test this offer, you must deploy these three operational safeguards:

* [ ] **The "One-In, One-Out" Rule:** If a team member takes on an administrative task for the new stream, they must automate or hand off an existing chore from the core channel.
* [ ] **The Tool Lockdown:** Do not buy new software licenses for this test. Use your existing stack (Typeform, Stripe, Google Drive) until the stream generates recurring revenue.
* [ ] **The Execution Sandbox:** Run the entire fulfillment process on a dedicated calendar block (e.g., Friday mornings only). If it bleeds into your primary core delivery hours, pull the plug.
